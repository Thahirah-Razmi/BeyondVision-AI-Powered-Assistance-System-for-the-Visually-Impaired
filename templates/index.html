Help & User Guide

Welcome to BeyondVision

BeyondVision is an AI-powered navigation and assistance system for visually impaired users. It combines real-time video analysis, voice guidance, map navigation, and text reading into one accessible platform.

Quick Start — 3 Easy Steps

Step What to Do What Happens
1 Open Settings ⚙️ (top-left) and choose Blind End or Family End mode The interface adapts to your role
2 Fill in your Profile (name, age, gender) in Settings Voice prompts become personalised to you
3 Upload a video, set a destination, read text, or use a voice command The system guides you with voice and visual feedback

What Can BeyondVision Do?

  • 🎯 Blind Track Analysis — Upload a video to detect tactile paving direction and receive spoken turn-by-turn prompts
  • 🗺️ Map Navigation — Search for a destination, plan a walking route, and get directions with voice guidance
  • 🎤 Voice Commands — Control the app hands-free by speaking commands like "Go to the park"
  • 📖 OCR Text Reader — Upload a photo of any text (labels, documents) and hear it read aloud
  • 🌤️ Environment Safety — Check weather and get a safety score before heading out
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 Family Monitoring — Family members can send voice messages to the user remotely

Blind Track Video Analysis

This feature analyses a video of tactile paving (blind tracks) and automatically announces voice directions when the path turns left, right, or goes straight.

How to Use

  1. In the Blind Track Video Analysis card, click "Select Video File" or drag and drop a video onto the upload area.
  2. Supported formats: MP4, AVI, MOV, MKV, WEBM (max 300 MB).
  3. Click "Start Analysis". The video uploads and processing begins.
  4. Watch the live analysis feed — detected blind tracks are highlighted in real time.
  5. Listen for voice prompts in the Voice Navigation Prompts panel. Directions are announced automatically when a turn is detected.

Understanding the Voice Prompts

Detection Result Voice Announcement
Path curves left "Please turn left"
Path curves right "Please turn right"
Path goes straight "Continue straight ahead"
No path detected No announcement (system waits)

Tips for Best Results

  • Film the blind track from a downward angle with the tactile tiles clearly visible.
  • Good lighting improves accuracy — avoid heavy shadows or glare.
  • The system re-evaluates direction roughly every 14 seconds to avoid repetitive prompts.
  • Adjust voice speed and volume in Settings → Voice Settings before starting.
  • While a video is analysing, Settings are temporarily disabled.

Family End — Sending Voice Messages

In Family End mode, a message input box appears below the Voice Navigation Prompts panel. Type a message and press Send (or Enter) to broadcast it as a voice message to the user's device in real time.

Location & Navigation

BeyondVision uses OpenStreetMap to show your location, find nearby blind tracks, and plan walking routes with spoken turn-by-turn directions.

Step 1 — Find Your Location

  • Click "Locate My Position" to detect your location via GPS.
  • If GPS fails, click More → Manually Select Current Location and tap your position on the map.
  • Your address appears in the Location Info panel below the map.

Step 2 — Set a Destination

  • Type a place name in the destination search bar and click 🔍.
  • Or use More → Manually Select Destination and click the map.
  • Or say "Go to [place name]" as a voice command.

Step 3 — Plan a Route

  • Click More → Plan Route to calculate a route from your position to the destination.
  • The route appears on the map in blue. Turn-by-turn directions appear in the Directions Panel.
  • Click "Start Navigation" to begin guided navigation with voice announcements.
  • Click "Stop" to end navigation at any time.

Finding Nearby Blind Tracks

  • Click More → Find Nearby Blind Tracks to search within 1.5 km of your location.
  • Found tracks are drawn on the map in green. Click any track for details and navigation.
  • Use the filter buttons (All / Nearby / Footpaths) to narrow the list.

Voice Commands

Control BeyondVision hands-free via the Voice Command panel at the top of the page.

How to Start

  1. Click "🎤 Start Voice Command". The button turns red when listening.
  2. Speak your command clearly.
  3. The system responds with a spoken confirmation and performs the action.
  4. Or use the quick-action buttons (Set Destination, Read Text, Detect Obstacles, Get Help) to skip speaking.

Tips & Troubleshooting

  • Speak at a clear, moderate pace. Say the command word first: "Go to Central Park", not "Central Park, go to".
  • Reduce background noise where possible.
  • Requires Chrome, Edge, or Safari with microphone permission enabled.
  • If the button shows "Not Supported", switch to a compatible browser.
  • If the browser asks for microphone access, click Allow.

Smart OCR Text Reader

Photograph any text — labels, documents — and have it read aloud instantly.

How to Use

  1. Scroll to the Smart OCR Text Reader section at the bottom of the page.
  2. Click "Select Image File" and choose a photo. Supported formats: JPEG, PNG, GIF, BMP.
  3. The image is submitted automatically on selection.
  4. Recognised text appears in the result box and is read aloud automatically.

You can also trigger this via voice command by saying "Read" or "Scan".

Tips for Better Recognition

  • Hold the camera steady and ensure text is in focus before shooting.
  • Use even lighting — avoid shadows and glare on the text.
  • For large documents, photograph one section at a time.
  • The system works best on printed text. Handwriting accuracy may vary.

Result Outcomes

Outcome What You See What You Hear
Text found Recognised text shown with character count Text is read aloud automatically
No text found "No text could be detected" message No audio — try a clearer image
Error Error message in red No audio — check file format and retry

User Modes & Settings

BeyondVision has two modes. Switch between them in Settings → Mode.

Blind End Mode

  • Receives all real-time voice navigation prompts and blind track direction guidance.
  • Receives voice messages sent by family members.
  • The family message input box is hidden to keep the interface clean.
  • All voice commands are active and optimised for hands-free use.

Family End Mode

  • Can view the live video analysis feed in real time.
  • A message input box appears — type a message and press Send (or Enter) to broadcast it as a voice message to the user immediately.
  • Can see the same map and location status as the visually impaired user.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why isn't GPS locating me?

Your browser may have blocked location access, or you may be indoors. Use More → Manually Select Current Location and tap your position on the map. Also check that location permissions are allowed for this site in your browser settings.

Why can't I use Settings during video analysis?

Settings are locked while a video is actively being processed to prevent disrupting the live stream. Wait for the System Status panel to show "Completed", then open Settings.

Voice commands aren't being recognised — what do I do?

Check that your browser has microphone permission. Voice commands require Chrome, Edge, or Safari. Speak clearly and reduce background noise. If the button shows "Not Supported", try a different browser.

No blind tracks showing on the map — why?

Blind track data may be incomplete in your area. The search radius is 1.5 km. Try setting your position manually for more precision, then search again.

OCR result is inaccurate or empty — what can I do?

Take a clearer, well-lit photo with the text fully in frame and in focus. Avoid shadows, glare, and tilted angles. Try retaking the photo in better lighting.

How do I send a voice message to the user?

Switch to Family End mode in Settings. A message input box appears below the Voice Navigation Prompts panel. Type your message and press Send or Enter — it plays on the user's device immediately.

Does this work on mobile?

Yes. BeyondVision is fully responsive. Voice commands and GPS both work on mobile. Use Chrome on Android or Safari on iOS for the best experience.

Route planning fails — what should I do?

Route planning requires an internet connection. Check your connection and try again. Also confirm your start location is set using Locate My Position or manual selection, then retry.
